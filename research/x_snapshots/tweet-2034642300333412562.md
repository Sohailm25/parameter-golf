        Day 1 on OpenAI's Parameter Golf challenge. Build the best language model that fits in 16MB, scored by compression on FineWeb. Ran 30 experiments overnight on my MacBook Air. Here's what I found.

Core idea: depth recurrence. Instead of 9 unique transformer layers, use 3 blocks and loop them 3x. Same depth, 1/3 the parameters. Use the freed budget for wider layers. First test beat the baseline
  immediately.

Iterated fast by evaluating on 10% of the validation set. Brought each experiement from 30 min down to 8 min.

Things that didn't work:
  • Smear gate + backout from the NanoGPT speedrun, made things worse
  • 2 unique layers, not enough diversity
  • More than 3 recurrences, diminishing returns

The pivot: wider models looked worse on 1 data shard. Thought something was broken. Turned out they were just data-starved. Scaled to 10 shards and everthing flipped.

  d1024 → 1.996 BPB
  d896 → 2.010
  d768 → 2.050

A 5000 step training got down to 1.589 BPB on a MacBook. Leaderboard baseline is 1.224 on 8xH100s. Gap should close with more data and compute.

Built an adaptive sweep system that analyzes past results and picks the next config to try. Ran 30 experiements overnight.

Next steps: Winning model compresses to 20MB so need to get it under 16MB. Trying QAT and finding the right size under budget. Then hopefully getting some H100 time.
